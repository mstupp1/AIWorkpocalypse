## Introduction (~2,500 words)
- Hook: modern work feels artificial, performative, output-driven
- Thesis: the AI era didn't break corporate work — it revealed what was already broken, and then turbocharged it
- Preview the four-part structure
- Set expectations: this is a survival guide, not a manifesto

## Part 1: The AI Work Layer (Why This Is Happening)

### Ch 1: The Great Normalization

- The floor rose, the ceiling blurred
- Everyone has the same tools now — what does differentiation even mean?

- 80% correct, 100% of the time
- The economics of caring less about the last 20%

### Ch 3: The Translation Layer
- AI as interpreter of vague human intent
- Converting ambiguity into deliverables
- The irony of building machines to understand us because we stopped trying ourselves

### Ch 4: The Arms Race
- OpenAI vs. Anthropic vs. Google — the acceleration
- Enterprise adoption lagging behind consumer adoption
- Corporate "AI strategy" as performance art

### Ch 5: The Collapse of Effort Visibility
- Output decoupled from effort
- Credit, attribution, and confusion
- Managers can't tell who's actually good anymore

- AI sidebars driving fake conversations in ticket comments
