# Surviving the AI Workpocalypse: A Cynic's Guide

**By Myles Stupp**

A satirical nonfiction book about how corporate work *actually* functions in the AI era — and how to navigate it without losing your mind, your job, or your will to live.

---

## What This Book Is

This is a guide for anyone who has ever:

- Sat through a meeting that could have been a Slack message
- Written documentation that nobody will ever read
- Watched AI generate in 30 seconds what used to take a team two weeks
- Been told to "do more with less" while the org hires three new VPs
- Wondered if anyone actually knows what's going on

If you've experienced any of the above, congratulations — you work in corporate America circa 2026. This book is for you.

## What This Book Is Not

- A corporate whitepaper
- A cheerful guide to "leveraging AI for productivity"
- A textbook
- Optimistic

## Structure

The book is organized into four parts:

| Part | Title | Focus |
|------|-------|-------|
| **1** | The AI Work Layer | Why the landscape shifted and what it means |
| **2** | The Reality of Corporate Work | How organizations actually function (vs. how they say they do) |
| **3** | Operating Inside the System | Practical strategies for navigating the machine |
| **4** | Stability, Survival & Positioning | How to stay employed, sane, and effective |
